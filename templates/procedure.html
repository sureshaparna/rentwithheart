Rental Guide

Beginners guide to renting in Victoria

1. Have your identification ready

This can be your license, passport, birth certificate, Medicare card, or your Visa letter if you are from any other country.

2. Have your references ready

This could be from your employer. Your previous house owner or someone whom you trust and who can refer you

3. Have your rental history ready.

This could be the rental for two years and give the contact of your previous house owners or landlords

4. Have your proof of income ready

This is not limited to proof of income but your employment letter or your payslips for the past six months as well

5. Have your employment details and history

Please ensure that you provide accurate and complete information about your employment details and history.

6. Have the copy of your bank statements

Make sure to keep a copy of your bank statements for your records and financial documentation purposes.

7. Have your recommendation letter from charities

Remember to obtain a recommendation letter from charities you have worked with to showcase your community involvement.
